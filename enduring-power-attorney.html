Enduring Power of Attorney

Making Your Personal Choices Known In Case of Incapacity

An Enduring Power of Attorney (EPOA) is a legal document that allows individuals to appoint specific people to manage specific affairs in the unlikely event of one’s incapacity while they are healthy and mentally competent. This legal ability was enabled by an amendment to the Law of Legal Capacity and Guardianship passed by the Knesset in 2017..

Under this law, you are empowered to appoint specific individuals to manage your specific future affairs in relation to your financial and property matters, everyday needs, and medical issues. End of life measures, however, are not covered by an Enduring Power of Attorney, and is instead covered by the ‘Advanced Medical Directive’ which is executed separately and in accordance with the guidelines set forth by the Ministry of Health. You can also nominate co-appointees and alternates to your main appointees. You are allowed to appoint individuals who reside outside of the State of Israel, but must do so with discretion and due consideration of the logistics involved in such an appointment.
