Notary Service

Helping You With Your Notarial Needs

All notary fees in the State of Israel are fixed by law, so there is no price competition between notaries. Every year, the Israeli Ministry of Justice publishes a 'Notary Notice', which outlines the costs associated with notary services in the State of Israel. The fixed fees, however, only cover the fee of the notary service itself and does not include any miscellaneous fees associated with the preparation of the paperwork to be authenticated by the notary public.

Our law firm is a one-stop shop, and can help you not only with your notary needs, but with your other legal requirements.

Our notarial services include, but are not limited to:

- Certification of translations in the a.m. languages
- Confirmation of signatures
- Confirmation of signatures on behalf of another person or on behalf of a company
- Certification of Copies
- Confirmation of affidavits
- Life certificates

Certification of Translations:
- NIS 213 for the first 100 words or a part thereof;
- NIS 169 for succeeding words after the first 100 words, up to 1,000 words or a part thereof;
- NIS 81 for succeeding words after the first 1,000 words or a part thereof

Confirmation of Signatures:
- NIS 169 for one person;
- NIS 67 for any additional person;
- NIS 232 for confirmation of signatures on behalf of another person, or on behalf of a company

Certification of Copies:
- NIS 67 for the first page;
- NIS 5 for succeeding pages after the first page

Apostille Service
When warranted, a notarized document may be Apostilled pursuant to the Hague Convention of 1961. This makes the apostilled document legally useable in a foreign jurisdiction that's a signatory of the Hague Convention.
